03 // QUESTIONS

FAQs: Computer Engineering in the Philippines

01

What is Computer Engineering?

+

Computer Engineering is a discipline that integrates several fields of electrical engineering and computer science required to develop computer hardware and software. It combines the principles of engineering with the power of computing to design, build, and optimize systems that drive modern technology.

02

How long does it take to complete Computer Engineering in the Philippines?

+

The Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering (BSCpE) program in the Philippines typically takes 4-5 years to complete, depending on the university and curriculum. The program includes theoretical coursework, laboratory sessions, and an On-the-Job Training (OJT) component.

03

What are the career opportunities for Computer Engineers in the Philippines?

+

Computer Engineers in the Philippines have diverse career opportunities including: Software Developer, Hardware Engineer, Network Engineer, Systems Analyst, Embedded Systems Developer, Data Engineer, Cybersecurity Specialist, AI/ML Engineer, and IT Project Manager. The growing tech industry in the Philippines offers competitive salaries both locally and internationally.

04

Is Computer Engineering in demand in the Philippines?

+

Yes, Computer Engineering is one of the most in-demand courses and professions in the Philippines. With the rapid digital transformation, rise of BPO industries, startup ecosystem growth, and increasing adoption of emerging technologies like AI, IoT, and cloud computing, the demand for skilled computer engineers continues to rise.

05

What is the difference between Computer Engineering and Computer Science?

+

Computer Engineering focuses on the design and development of computer hardware and embedded systems, combining electrical engineering with computer science. Computer Science primarily deals with software development, algorithms, and theoretical foundations of computing. Computer Engineers work closer to the hardware level, while Computer Scientists focus more on software and applications.

06

Do I need to take the board exam for Computer Engineering?

+

Yes, graduates of Computer Engineering in the Philippines are required to take and pass the Computer Engineering Licensure Examination conducted by the Professional Regulation Commission (PRC) to practice as a Registered Computer Engineer. The exam covers various topics including mathematics, electronics, programming, and professional ethics.

07

What skills do I need to succeed in Computer Engineering?

+

Key skills for Computer Engineering success include: strong mathematical and analytical abilities, programming proficiency (C++, Python, Java), problem-solving skills, understanding of electronics and circuits, attention to detail, continuous learning mindset, teamwork and communication skills, and adaptability to new technologies.

08

What is ICpEP.SE and why should I join?

+

ICpEP.SE (Institute of Computer Engineers of the Philippines - Student Edition) is the premier organization for Computer Engineering students in the Philippines. Joining provides networking opportunities, access to industry events like CPECON, professional development workshops, mentorship programs, and a community of like-minded individuals passionate about technology and innovation.
